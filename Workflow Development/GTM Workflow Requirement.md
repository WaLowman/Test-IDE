## GTM Strategy

Highveld-GTS is an existing company, but it's expanding into Authentic Cultural Experiences. It now wants to use artificial intelligence to develop an agentic go-to-market process enshrined in an agentic workflow. The workflow breaks out into four stages:

1. Discovery
2. Ranking
3. Research
4. Outreach

## GTM Pillars

We have identified three pillars for our go-to-market strategy.

### Clients

These are the organisations, groups and individuals that 'pay' for the experiences.

The can be identified as follows:

1. Organisations that have international visitors and/or have to entertain local guests:

- Companies
- NGOs
- Government and its agencies
- International bodies (UN, World Bank, AU, etc.)
- Embassies and diplomatic missions
- Educational institutions (universities, business schools, etc.)

2. Events that bring international visitors and/or local guests to Gauteng

- Conferences
- Big entertainment events (sports, music, etc.)
- International product launches (automotive, tech, etc.)
- Trade shows and expos
- Diplomatic summits and bilateral meetings

3. Individual business travellers and professionals

- Executives with free evenings or half-days
- Travel management companies and corporate travel desks (booking on behalf of individuals)
- Visiting academics and researchers

### Partners

These are organisations, groups and individuals that can refer paying clients to us.

They can be identified as follows:

1. Accommodation sector

- Accommodation providers (hotels, guest houses, etc.)
- Staff at accommodation providers (concierges, front desk, guest relations, etc.)

2. Events sector

- Hosts of conferences, events and product launches
- Organisers and suppliers to conferences, events and product launches (events organisers, PR agencies, etc.)

3. Travel and tourism trade

- Destination management companies (DMCs)
- Corporate travel managers, PAs and EA networks
- Airlines and business-class lounge partnerships
- Online travel platforms (Viator, GetYourGuide, TripAdvisor Experiences, etc.)

4. Institutional and diplomatic

- Tourism boards (Gauteng Tourism, SA Tourism, etc.)
- Chambers of commerce and business associations (AmCham, EUNIC, British Chamber, etc.)
- Embassy cultural attachés and protocol offices

### Experiences

These are organisations, groups and individuals that offer experiences that clients are interested in and willing to pay for.

They can be identified as follows:

- Culinary: restaurants, chefs, creators, consultants, suppliers, farmers, food markets, etc.
- Music: venues, musicians, booking agencies, etc.
- Sports: clubs, teams, athletes, stadiums, etc.
- Visual and performing arts: galleries, artists, agents, theatres, performing arts venues, etc.
- Literary: publishers, book stores, authors, agents, literary festivals, etc.
- Architecture: building owners, architects, professors, urban design studios, etc.
- History: historians, retirees, museums, heritage sites, etc.
- Township and community: community leaders, storytellers, local guides, homestay hosts, etc.
- Political and civic: heritage foundations, memorial sites, constitutional landmarks, etc.
- Nature and outdoors: botanical gardens, nature reserves, Cradle of Humankind, etc.
- Nightlife and entertainment: rooftop bars, jazz clubs, comedy venues, etc.
- Markets and shopping: craft markets, flea markets, design districts, etc.
- Fashion and design: designers, studios, showrooms, design schools, etc.
