- [ ] Milestone 1: Tourism registration and compliance complete — All regulatory registrations (Gauteng Tourism Authority, guide registrations, BBBEE certification) finalised
- [ ] Milestone 2: Insurance and safety protocols in place — R10M public liability cover obtained, vehicles equipped, emergency protocols established
- [ ] Milestone 3: Website and booking system live — Fully functional website with online booking, payment processing, and automated confirmations
- [ ] Milestone 4: All 3 signature experiences designed and tested — Mandela Legacy, Johannesburg After Dark, and The Spice Route itineraries completed with successful dry runs
- [ ] Milestone 5: Hotel concierge partnerships established — Concierge relationships active at all 5 target hotels with brochures placed and booking lines operational
- [ ] Milestone 6: Concierge familiarisation experiences hosted — Concierge staff from target hotels have completed complimentary ACE outings
- [ ] Milestone 7: Marketing assets and channels ready — Website, social media, brochures, photography, and corporate sales materials all live
- [ ] Milestone 8: Launch Day — First paid experience delivered successfully
- [ ] Milestone 9: Break-even achieved — 5 experiences per month sustained (target: Month 6)
- [ ] Milestone 10: Year 1 target reached — 100 experiences delivered

## 4. Project Risks (for Risk Log)

| Risk Description | Potential Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation Strategy |
|-----------------|-----------------|------------|-------------------|
| Concierge adoption rate lower than expected — hotels may be slow to recommend an unproven operator | High | Medium | Invest in familiarisation experiences, ensure prompt commission payments, build direct booking channels as fallback |
| Safety incident during an experience — injury, vehicle accident, or security event | High | Low | Maintain R10M insurance, enforce strict vehicle maintenance, equip first aid kits, establish emergency protocols, brief all staff |
| Guide availability or reliability — Praveen or Thandi's grandmother unavailable for a booking | Medium | Medium | Build pool of backup guides, cross-train team members on key narratives, maintain flexible scheduling |
| Seasonal demand fluctuations — low periods between conferences and peak travel seasons | Medium | High | Implement dynamic pricing, target corporate retainers for baseline revenue, diversify conference partnerships |
| Venue access disrupted — closures, renovations, or changed terms at key experience locations | Medium | Medium | Maintain contingency venues for each itinerary, design flexible routes with alternative stops |
| Negative online review or reputation damage — a poor experience amplified on TripAdvisor or Google | High | Low | Implement rapid response protocol, send proactive feedback surveys, conduct mystery-guest quality audits |
| Cash flow pressure during ramp-up — fixed costs exceed revenue before booking volume builds | High | Medium | Leverage existing transport business revenue, minimise fixed costs, maintain contingency fund, target Month 6 break-even |
| Regulatory changes to tourism registration requirements | Low | Low | Monitor regulatory environment proactively, maintain relationships with Gauteng Tourism Authority |
| Competition from established tour operators entering the premium niche | Medium | Medium | Differentiate on authenticity and personal connections, leverage AI for bespoke experiences, protect community relationships |
| Traffic and logistics delays affecting experience quality | Medium | High | Use AI-assisted route optimisation, build buffer time into itineraries, maintain flexible scheduling |
