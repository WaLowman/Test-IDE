## Customer Segments

**Primary segments**

- **International business executives** -- Senior professionals visiting Johannesburg for meetings, deal-making, or site visits. Typically staying 2--5 nights in Sandton hotels, with free evenings or half-day gaps. High disposable income (corporate expense accounts). Value convenience, exclusivity, and authentic local flavour over mass-market tourism. Estimated addressable pool: 50,000+ international business visitors to Gauteng per year via OR Tambo (21 million passengers annually).
- **Government and diplomatic delegates** -- Officials attending summits, bilateral meetings, and state visits. The G20 Summit alone brings 30,000+ delegates, officials, media, and support staff. Protocol-conscious, often require private or small-group arrangements. Interested in heritage and legacy experiences (particularly the Mandela narrative). Bookings often made by embassy staff or event organisers on their behalf.
- **International conference attendees** -- Visitors to events at the Sandton Convention Centre and similar venues. Shorter windows of free time (typically one evening). More price-sensitive than the executive segment but still on corporate budgets. Group bookings common (colleagues attending together).

**Secondary segments**

- **Corporates hosting international clients or partners** -- South African companies entertaining visiting counterparts. Book as part of corporate hospitality. Larger group sizes, repeat bookings likely. Decision-maker is a local PA, office manager, or events coordinator.
- **Incentive travel groups** -- International teams visiting as a reward or team-building exercise. Organised through travel management companies or HR departments. Prefer group experiences with a "wow" factor.
- **Long-term expatriates** -- Foreign residents in Johannesburg wanting to explore the city beyond their usual circuits. Lower urgency but recurring demand. Likely to discover the offering through word of mouth or social media rather than hotel concierges.

## Value Propositions

**Core value proposition:** Authentic, curated cultural experiences in Johannesburg designed specifically for time-poor international visitors -- combining premium transport with local insider knowledge in a single, seamless booking.

- **Authenticity over tourism** -- Experiences led by people with genuine personal connections to the places and stories (Praveen's Fordsburg heritage, Thandi's grandmother's Soweto roots, Boitumelo's intimate knowledge of the city). This is not a hop-on-hop-off bus; it is a personal introduction to Johannesburg by people who live its stories.
- **Time-optimised for business schedules** -- Packages designed around the reality of a business visitor's diary: 3-hour evening slots, 4-hour half-day windows, or full-day immersions. Pick-up and drop-off at the guest's hotel, with no wasted time on logistics.
- **End-to-end convenience** -- Transport, guide, entry fees, food and beverages, and gratuities bundled into a single price. One booking, one point of contact, no coordination required by the guest or their PA.
- **Safety and trust** -- Premium vehicles with professional drivers who know the city. A credible, insured operator (public liability minimum R10 million) removing the anxiety international visitors feel about navigating Johannesburg independently.
- **Personalisation** -- Itineraries tailored to guest interests, cultural background, dietary requirements, and available time. AI-assisted itinerary generation enables bespoke experiences without requiring a large planning team.
- **Small-group exclusivity** -- Maximum group sizes of 8--12 depending on the experience. No large coach tours. Intimate, conversational encounters with the city.
- **Cultural depth with contemporary relevance** -- Experiences that connect Johannesburg's heritage (Mandela legacy, Fordsburg's immigrant history, Soweto's liberation story) to its present-day creative energy (Maboneng arts district, live jazz scene, street food culture).
- **Corporate-ready packaging** -- Invoicing, BBBEE certificates, and professional booking confirmations that satisfy corporate procurement and expense reporting requirements.

## Channels

**Awareness channels (how guests discover ACEs)**

- **5-star and 4-star hotel concierge desks** -- The single most important channel. Business visitors rely on the concierge for local recommendations. Target hotels: Saxon Hotel, Four Seasons The Westcliff, Fairlawns Boutique Hotel, Sandton Sun, Radisson Blu Sandton. Physical brochures at concierge desks plus a direct booking line for concierge staff.
- **Corporate travel managers and PAs** -- The people who actually organise schedules for visiting executives. Reached through direct sales outreach, email campaigns, and LinkedIn networking. Once onboarded, they become a repeat referral source.
- **Conference and event organisers** -- Pre-event partnership with Sandton Convention Centre and major conference organisers to include ACEs in delegate welcome packs or as an official "social programme" option.
- **Embassy and consulate networks** -- For government delegates, the protocol and logistics teams at embassies coordinate visitor schedules. Direct relationship-building with key embassies (particularly G20 member nations).
- **Professional website and social media** -- A clean, mobile-optimised website with experience descriptions, pricing, and instant booking. Instagram and LinkedIn for visual storytelling and corporate credibility respectively.

**Evaluation and purchase channels (how guests book)**

- **Direct booking via website** -- Online booking with immediate confirmation, credit card or invoice payment options.
- **Concierge booking on behalf of guests** -- Concierge phones or emails a dedicated booking line; confirmation sent to both concierge and guest within 30 minutes.
- **Corporate account booking** -- Pre-agreed rates for corporate clients, booked by PAs or travel coordinators via email or a simple online portal. Monthly invoicing.
- **WhatsApp Business** -- For quick enquiries and last-minute bookings, particularly from guests already in Johannesburg.

**Delivery channel**

- **In-person, door-to-door** -- Premium vehicle collects the guest from their hotel, the experience unfolds, and the guest is returned. The vehicle and driver are the delivery platform.

**Post-experience channels**

- **Post-experience feedback survey** -- Sent via email or WhatsApp within 24 hours. Feeds into Net Promoter Score tracking and online review generation.
- **Review platforms** -- Encourage satisfied guests to leave reviews on TripAdvisor, Google, and specialist platforms. These reviews become a powerful awareness channel for future guests.
- **Referral programme** -- Encourage corporate clients and concierges to refer new bookings through a commission or incentive structure.

## Customer Relationships

**Dedicated personal assistance (primary segments)**

- **Single point of contact for corporate accounts** -- Each corporate client and hotel concierge partner is assigned a named contact within Highveld-GTS (initially Joanne). All bookings, queries, and post-experience follow-up go through this person. Business visitors and their PAs expect a human they can reach, not a generic inbox.
- **Pre-experience consultation** -- For custom and VIP bookings (R8,000+), a brief phone or WhatsApp call to understand the guest's interests, dietary needs, mobility considerations, and cultural preferences. This personal touch differentiates from mass-market operators and feeds directly into AI-assisted itinerary personalisation.

**Concierge-mediated relationship (hotel channel)**

- **The concierge is the customer** -- In many cases, the guest never contacts Highveld-GTS directly. The relationship is with the concierge, who recommends the experience. This means investing in the concierge relationship: regular check-ins, commission payments processed promptly, and ensuring every guest experience reflects well on the concierge's recommendation.
- **Concierge familiarisation experiences** -- Invite concierge staff from target hotels on complimentary ACE outings so they can speak from first-hand experience when recommending to guests.

**Automated and self-service (scale elements)**

- **Online booking with instant confirmation** -- For standard packages (Evening Experience, Half-Day Cultural), guests or PAs can book directly via the website without needing to speak to anyone. Automated confirmation email with itinerary details, driver name, and pick-up time.
- **Post-experience automated feedback loop** -- Automated email or WhatsApp message 24 hours after the experience with a short satisfaction survey and a link to leave a TripAdvisor or Google review.

**Community and co-creation**

- **Guest storytelling** -- With permission, share guest photos and testimonials on social media and the website. International business visitors become ambassadors for the brand in their home markets.
- **Repeat guest recognition** -- For returning visitors or corporate accounts with multiple bookings, offer a "returning friend" experience -- a new itinerary that builds on what they have already seen, creating a sense of progression and deepening connection to the city.

**Relationship cost profile:**

- Personal assistance (Joanne's time, phone calls, WhatsApp) -- low cash cost, high time cost; sustainable at low volumes but will need additional support beyond approximately 15 bookings per week
- Concierge familiarisation -- estimated R1,500--R2,500 per concierge outing (cost price), offset by the booking volume each concierge generates
- Automated systems (website, email sequences) -- upfront development cost, near-zero marginal cost per booking

## Revenue Streams

**Primary revenue stream: Experience package fees**

Per-person pricing for each package tier:

| Package | Duration | Price per person | Min group | Max group | Revenue per booking (range) |
|---------|----------|-----------------|-----------|-----------|----------------------------|
| Evening Experience | 3--4 hours | R1,500--R2,500 | 2 | 12 | R3,000--R30,000 |
| Half-Day Cultural | 4--5 hours | R2,000--R3,500 | 2 | 8 | R4,000--R28,000 |
| Full-Day Immersion | 8--10 hours | R4,000--R6,000 | 2 | 8 | R8,000--R48,000 |
| Custom/VIP | Flexible | R8,000+ | 1 | 6 | R8,000--R48,000+ |

Pricing model: all-inclusive per-person fee covering transport, guide, entry fees, food and beverages, and gratuities. No hidden extras.

**Peak-period pricing:** During the G20 Summit and other major conferences, prices increase by 15--25 per cent to reflect higher demand and tighter scheduling logistics. Target margin rises from 35--40 per cent to 45--50 per cent during peak periods.

**Secondary revenue stream: Corporate account retainers**

- Pre-agreed rates for companies with regular international visitors (target margin 30--35 per cent, offset by volume and predictability)
- Quarterly or annual framework agreements with guaranteed minimum bookings
- Estimated value: R15,000--R50,000 per corporate account per year (based on 5--15 bookings annually)

**Tertiary revenue stream: Reciprocal venue referral commissions**

- If Highveld-GTS refers guests to partner restaurants or venues outside the package, there is potential for a reciprocal commission of R50--R150 per referral from the venue

**Year 1 volume and revenue estimates:**

- Target: 100 experiences delivered in Year 1
- Average booking: 4 guests at an average price of R2,500 per person = R10,000 per booking
- Gross revenue estimate: R1,000,000
- Average blended margin (35 per cent): approximately R350,000 gross profit
- Break-even target: Month 6

**Payment methods:**

- Credit card (online booking) -- immediate settlement
- Electronic funds transfer (corporate accounts) -- 30-day terms
- Foreign currency cards accepted via payment gateway (international guests booking directly)

## Key Activities

**Experience design and curation**

- **Itinerary development** -- Research, design, and test each signature experience (The Mandela Legacy, Johannesburg After Dark, The Spice Route) including timing, route sequencing, venue selection, and contingency options (weather, venue closures, traffic).
- **Custom itinerary generation** -- Use AI tools to create personalised itineraries for VIP and bespoke bookings based on guest preferences, available time, group size, and cultural interests.
- **Experience quality testing** -- Conduct dry runs of each experience before launch, then periodic mystery-guest reviews to maintain standards.

**Operations and delivery**

- **Booking management** -- Receive, confirm, and schedule bookings across all channels (website, concierge, corporate accounts, WhatsApp). Coordinate driver, guide, and venue availability for each booking.
- **Guest logistics** -- Confirm pick-up times and locations, communicate itinerary details to guests 24 hours in advance, manage day-of coordination between driver and guide.
- **Route optimisation** -- Plan efficient routes that maximise experience time and minimise transit, accounting for Johannesburg traffic patterns. AI-assisted route planning for custom itineraries.
- **Safety management** -- Maintain vehicle roadworthiness, ensure first aid kits are stocked, brief drivers and guides on emergency protocols, and monitor safety conditions at experience locations.

**Partner and venue management**

- **Venue and community partner onboarding** -- Negotiate access agreements, pricing, and availability with restaurants, cultural venues, museums, and community hosts. Establish service-level expectations.
- **Guide recruitment and training** -- Onboard cultural guides (starting with Praveen and Thandi's grandmother), provide hospitality training, and ensure tourism registration compliance with the Department of Tourism.
- **Hotel concierge relationship management** -- Regular visits to concierge desks, commission tracking and payment, familiarisation experience invitations, and feedback sharing.

**Marketing and sales**

- **Content creation** -- AI-assisted production of marketing materials: experience descriptions, photography (Boitumelo), social media posts, website content, and hotel brochure inserts.
- **Multi-language communication** -- AI-assisted translation of guest communications, booking confirmations, and marketing materials for key international markets.
- **Corporate sales outreach** -- Direct engagement with corporate travel managers, PAs, and event organisers to secure corporate accounts.
- **Online presence management** -- Website maintenance, social media posting, TripAdvisor and Google review management, and search engine visibility.

**Compliance and administration**

- **Tourism registration** -- Register with the Gauteng Tourism Authority and ensure ongoing compliance.
- **Insurance management** -- Maintain public liability cover (minimum R10 million) and vehicle insurance.
- **BBBEE certification** -- Obtain and maintain Level 4 or better BBBEE status for corporate client eligibility.
- **Financial administration** -- Invoicing, expense tracking, commission payments to hotels, guide fee payments, and margin monitoring.

## Cost Structure

**Cost model characterisation:** Value-driven. ACEs compete on authenticity, personalisation, and convenience rather than price. The cost structure supports premium quality, not minimum expense.

**Fixed costs (incurred regardless of bookings)**

- Joanne's time allocation (portion of existing salary): R8,000--R12,000 per month
- Insurance (public liability, minimum R10 million): R3,000--R5,000 per month
- Website and booking system (hosting, domain, payment gateway): R500--R1,500 per month
- Marketing baseline (social media, brochures, photography): R3,000--R5,000 per month
- Tourism registration and BBBEE certification: R5,000--R10,000 per year
- AI tool subscriptions: R1,000--R2,000 per month
- Mobile phone and communication: R500--R1,000 per month

**Estimated total fixed costs: R16,000--R26,500 per month (R192,000--R318,000 per year)**

- Website design and development: R15,000--R25,000

- Contingency: R10,000--R15,000

**Estimated total launch investment: R50,000--R75,000**

**Break-even analysis:**

- Average gross profit per experience (4 guests, blended): approximately R4,500
- Monthly fixed costs (mid-range): approximately R21,000
- Break-even volume: approximately 5 experiences per month
- Year 1 target of 100 experiences = approximately 8--9 per month, comfortably above break-even once ramped up
