# Scoring Model

Use a fast, pragmatic scoring model out of `25`.

## Scoring Criteria

### 1. Audience Fit `0-5`

How directly does the target serve business, diplomatic, or international visitors in Gauteng?

- `Score 1`: weak fit. Audience is mostly local, leisure-only, or unrelated to business, diplomatic, or international visitor segments.
- `Score 3`: moderate fit. Some overlap with relevant visitor segments, but mixed focus or limited evidence of consistent relevance.
- `Score 5`: strong fit. Clear and direct focus on business, diplomatic, or international visitors in Gauteng, with visible alignment to their needs.

### 2. Buying Power Or Influence `0-5`

Can this target buy, approve, refer, or shape repeat demand?

- `Score 1`: low influence. No clear budget authority, referral role, or decision-making leverage.
- `Score 3`: partial influence. Can influence or refer in some cases, but authority is indirect, occasional, or constrained.
- `Score 5`: high influence. Has direct buying power, approval authority, or strong referral capacity that can drive repeat demand.

### 3. Experience Match `0-5`

Is there a natural fit for curated transport-plus-culture offers?

- `Score 1`: weak match. Offer is hard to package, poorly aligned, or unlikely to resonate with target audience expectations.
- `Score 3`: workable match. Some clear fit, but adaptations, tighter positioning, or extra proof may be needed.
- `Score 5`: strong match. Natural, compelling fit for curated transport-plus-culture offers with clear customer appeal.

### 4. Ease Of Access `0-5`

Is there a visible route-in, named contact, active programme, or accessible channel?

- `Score 1`: difficult access. No named contact, no clear route-in, and limited public channels to engage.
- `Score 3`: moderate access. Some route-in exists (for example, generic inbox, occasional events, second-degree intro), but conversion path is uncertain.
- `Score 5`: easy access. Named decision-maker or active channel is available, with a clear and practical path to conversation.

### 5. Timing Signal `0-5`

Is there an immediate reason to engage now, such as events, visitor flows, hosted delegations, seasonal demand, or visible partnership activity?

- `Score 1`: weak timing. No clear near-term trigger; engagement is likely to be low urgency.
- `Score 3`: moderate timing. Some current activity or seasonal relevance, but urgency is not yet strong.
- `Score 5`: strong timing. Clear immediate trigger (events, delegations, peak flows, or active partnership moves) that supports outreach now.

## Priority Bands

- `21-25`: pursue now
- `16-20`: strong follow-up
- `11-15`: hold for later
- `0-10`: archive unless new evidence appears

## Tie-Break Rules

If two targets score the same, prefer:

1. stronger audience fit
2. clearer route-in
3. shorter path to a pilot or intro

## Pillar-Specific Notes

### Clients

Weight buying authority and visitor relevance more heavily in the rationale.

### Partners

Weight referral potential and packaging compatibility more heavily in the rationale.

### Experiences

Weight distinctiveness, operational feasibility, and premium sellability more heavily in the rationale.
